## Use this when

Writing or editing any text on the site — bio, page headlines, meta descriptions, CTA labels, or any copy.

---

## Voice

**Grounded, present, world-aware.** Not hype. Not marketing-speak. Not humble either.

The band's own words: *"We are in the world, not of the world, but we reflect the world around us."*

Write as if the band is speaking directly — confident, intentional, unhurried.

---

## Tone rules

- Short sentences over long ones
- Active over passive
- Concrete over abstract
- Don't oversell. Let the music speak.
- No exclamation points unless quoting press
- No "check out" — just link or name the thing

---

## Name and capitalization

- **Verdèzul** — always with the accent, always one word, never hyphenated
- **ETASOBAG** — all caps (it's an acronym: Earthtones and Shades of Blue and Green)
- **Arts-Link** — hyphenated, internal only (doesn't appear on the client-facing site)

---

## Meta descriptions

- 100–160 characters
- State what the page is, not what it promises
- Include "Verdèzul" and the primary topic

Good: `"Verdèzul tour dates and live show info — Los Angeles-based hip-hop duo. Buy tickets."`
Bad: `"Check out Verdèzul's amazing upcoming tour dates and grab your tickets before they sell out!"`

---

## CTAs

| Context | Use | Don't use |
|---------|-----|----------|
| Tickets | "Get Tickets", "Buy Tickets" | "Grab Tickets!", "Snag your spot" |
| Music | "Listen on Spotify", "Watch on YouTube" | "Stream now!", "Check it out" |
| Shop | "Buy", "Shop" | "Get yours before they're gone" |
| Contact | "Get in touch", "Send a message" | "Hit us up", "Reach out!" |
| Email | "Stay in the loop", "Join the list" | "Subscribe now!", "Don't miss out" |

---

## What to avoid

- Hype language: "incredible", "amazing", "epic", "fire" (unless quoting someone)
- Generic band-site copy: "Join us on this musical journey"
- Fake urgency: "Limited time", "Act now"
- Vet or authority language (this isn't chill-dogs, but same principle — don't overclaim)
