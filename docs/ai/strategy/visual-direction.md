## Use this when

Making any design or layout decision — choosing imagery, writing CSS, selecting layout modules, or evaluating whether something fits the brand.

---

## Core aesthetic

**Wilderness + earthtones + editorial darkness**

The site should feel like being in the field — raw, grounded, alive. Not polished in a commercial way. Think: hunting light, forest canopy, soil, water, the globe as a living thing.

**Verdèzul** = green + blue = the natural world, life itself. The band reflects the world around them without being of it.

| Site | What to take from it |
|------|---------------------|

| txdxe.com | Raw imagery, high contrast, editorial confidence |
| golfwang.com | Unconventional layout energy, color used deliberately |
| travisscott.com | Full-bleed visuals, immersive hero, dark palette |

**Common thread:** These are not typical band template sites. They are editorial, confident, and dark. Avoid anything that looks like a theme out of the box.

## Color usage

| Color | Role |
|-------|------|
| Black | Primary background — default state |
| White | Primary text on dark backgrounds |
| Green (`#2d5a27` range) | Primary brand — use for accents, CTAs, highlights |
| Blue (`#1a3a6b` range) | Secondary brand — complements green, used in gradients and accents |
| Red | Sparingly — alert states, strong accent moments only |
| Brown / Ochre | Earthy warm tones — background textures, photo overlays |

Exact hex values TBD pending client asset delivery. Use these directional ranges until confirmed.

## Typography direction

- Bold, weighted headlines — the band name and section titles should command space
- Clean, readable body text — legibility over decoration
- Avoid script fonts, avoid anything that reads as "country" or "folk" — this is hip-hop/rap adjacent

---

## Imagery direction

- Nature: forests, fields, sky, water, earth
- Outdoor / wilderness settings for band photography
- High contrast — shadows matter
- Avoid: studio backdrops, generic performance photos, anything stock-looking

---

## Do not do

- Bright, colorful, poppy layouts
- Gradient abuse
- Rounded corner excess
- Serif body text
- Stock photography
- Anything that looks like a WordPress theme or SquareSpace template
- Generic "band website" patterns (hero slider with tour dates overlay, etc.)
